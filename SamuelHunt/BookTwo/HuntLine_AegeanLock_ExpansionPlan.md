# Hunt Line Book Two – Expansion Plan (Aegean Lock)

## Goal
Deliver ~85k-word sequel set in Greece with 20 chapters + epilogue, matching the hybrid Lee Child/Tom Clancy tone established in Book One.

## Structure
- **Acts:** 4 acts (Parthenon Shadows, Island Killbox, Siege of Crete, Mykonos Reckoning) + Epilogue.
- **Target Word Count:** ~4.2k per chapter, ~2.5k for epilogue (~85k total ≈ 350 print pages).

## Chapter Development Checklist
1. **Cold Open (300–500 words):** Scene hook tied to iconic Greek location.
2. **Intel Layer (1k words):** Clancy-style detail (NIS protocols, NATO base schematics, maritime traffic).
3. **Action Beat (1.5–2k words):** Reacher-brutal close combat or tactical chase.
4. **Character Beat (800 words):** Dialogue advancing relationships (Hunt/Leyla/Noura/Daphne).
5. **Cliffhanger (200 words):** Transition into next chapter.

## Interludes
- **Maya Briefings:** Strategic view of NATO/EU response.
- **Maverick Monologues:** Propaganda broadcasts referencing Greek mythology.
- **Daphne Field Notes:** NIS perspective.
- **Jenny’s Logbook:** Sailing intel; adds flavor and technical detail.

## Deliverables
- `HuntLine_AegeanLock_Manuscript.md` – master text file.
- `kindle/` packaging folder (later).
- Optional research appendices (maps, glossary).

## Immediate Tasks
1. Draft Act I Chapters 1–5 (~20k words) in manuscript file.
2. Re-evaluate outline before Act II to incorporate new intel.
3. Once Act I done, prep Kindle build skeleton for quick EPUB generation.
